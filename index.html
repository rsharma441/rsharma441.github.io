Top Music of 2023
 
Hey all,
Once again, it's time to see what I thought were among the best songs and albums this year. I felt very grateful this year, because I got to travel the world and meet so many incredible people. Amidst the noise of social media tailoring our experiences and mainstream media profiting from division, this year's travels underscored how fundamentally alike we all are. Music, however, eloquently speaks of our profound, unexplainable connections. To those who made this year extraordinary and to the magic of music—my heartfelt thanks to each of you. Here is a link to last year's!
 
I'll also mention that these times continue to test us and that it's even more important for us to shed any negative presumptions about seeking help, both through a loved one or professionals. Anyway, here's looking to an even better 2024, and on to the top songs of 2023!

Top Albums of 2023

And here they are, starting with #25. You can click the Album image to get a Spotify/YouTube Link. Also, the email gets cut off so you'll have to click "View entired message"
25. WOW - Kate NV

(experimental/pop) The Moscow experimental artists departs from her previous more legible works, this album showcases dense, hyper-saturated compositions blending gaudy synths, field recordings, and manipulated vocals. It invokes heavy maximalism with unconventional sound bringing the listener into oblique and playful spaces. Great if you like: Kelly Lee Owens, Lucrecia Dalt, Jenny Hval

 
24. Black Rainbows - Corinne Bailey Rae

(rock/r&b) A Corinne Bailey Rae, known for easy listening tunes, shifted to unabashed rock music with her album "Black Rainbows." The change from jazz, soul, and R&B to scuzzy, guitar-powered rock marks a sharp departure from her previous style with great confidence. Great if you like: David Bowie, Prince, Radiohead

 
23. After the Magic - Parannoul (파란노을)

(experimental/indie rock) Parannoul's 'After the Magic' is a genre-blurring album, fusing nostalgic electronic tones and emotive guitar melodies to create a transformative musical experience that defies convention and seamlessly traverses genres. Great if you like: Panda Bear, Grimes, Smashing Pumpkins

 
22. Jam City Presents EFM - Jam City

(electronic) EFM by Jam City merges garage, disco, and house into an electric celebration of the nightlife's irresistible magnetism. It dances between euphoric club tracks and emotive tones, crafting a diverse sonic voyage. Great if you like: Daft Punk, Aphex Twins, Sophie, Arca

 
21. Santhosam - Priya Ragu

(pop/r&b) Priya Ragu, a Tamil Swiss singer, aims to portray her multi-layered experiences in her music, blending R&B, rap, and Tamil folk into a style she terms "Ragu-wavy." Her album, "Santhosam," delves into diverse themes from familial pressures to addressing police brutality. Drawing from her South Asian roots, she infuses influences from Tamil folk music, creating vibrant tracks that exude self-determined optimism and confidence, albeit occasionally lacking depth in addressing complex experiences. Great if you like: Dua Lipa, Rihanna, M.I.A 

 
20. Hearth Room - Frost Children

(electronic/rock) - Frost Children, known for their chaotic, maximalist approach to hyperpop, undergo a transformative shift in their album "Hearth Room." Departing from their aggressive, meme-filled style, they embrace a gentler sound, incorporating conventional pop structures and emotional depth. The album showcases a mix of romantic disillusionment, tender synth-pop melodies, and absurdist humor, culminating in a more emotionally resonant musical direction for the duo. Great if you like: Modest Mouse, 100 gecs, Alex G, Charli XCX

 
19. Flowers at Your Feet - Rahill

(pop, r&b)  Rahill Jamalifard, previously a member of the band Habibi, ventures into a contemplative and introspective solo debut with "Flowers At Your Feet." Departing from her garage-rock roots, this album showcases her Iranian heritage, personal memories, and love for family. It's an intimate exploration of childhood, family, and cultural influences, drawing upon Rahill's multicultural background to create a nostalgic yet contemporary sound. Great if you like: St. Vincent. FKA Twigs, Beck

 
18. Work of Art- Asake

(ampiano/afro-beat) Asake's second album, "Work of Art," highlights his confidence and success, blending amapiano with Afrofusion. He's established a unique sound by incorporating Yoruba elements and blending them with choral harmonies and string arrangements. With tracks like "Basquiat" and "Sunshine," he celebrates his achievements while emphasizing communal joy. His music, drawing from African roots and contemporary beats, echoes a narrative of rebirth and innovation. Great if you like: Burna Boy, Davido, Olamide, Fela Kuti 

 
17. In The End it Always Does - Japanese House

(pop/r&b)  Collaborating with George Daniel and Chloe Kraemer, Bain crafts a narrative of lust, despair, and resilience amid breakup, infusing each track with vibrant energy and heartfelt lyrics that reflect moments of playful experimentation and. Great if you like: The 1975, Bon Iver, MUNA, Charli XCX

 
16. Valley Of Heart's Delight - Margo Cilker

(folk/country) Valley of Heart’s Delight, Margo Cilker's follow-up to the acclaimed Pohorylle, revisits the same Americana sound that earned praise for its simplicity and evocative storytelling. Recorded with familiar musicians and producer Sera Cahoone, the album channels nostalgia for California's past while embracing a fuller musical landscape, yet occasionally slows in its pacing. Despite this, the album remains authentic, narrating tales of personal growth and redemption with engaging, heartfelt delivery. Great if you like: Jason Isbell, Margo Price, Sturgill Simpson, John Prine

 

15. Mother Road - Grace Potter

(folk, rock) Grace Potter's musical journey has always embraced a free-bird ethos, evident in her transition from the rootsy rock of her earlier band to her current solo venture. Her latest album, "Mother Road," draws inspiration from personal experiences, including childhood escapades and a recent cross-country adventure. Through vibrant characters and stories, Potter celebrates the diverse paths we navigate, both physically and spiritually, while encountering a colorful cast of characters along the way. Great if you like: Brandi Carlile, Sheryl Crow, Tedeschi Trucks Band, Haim, Alabama Shakes

 
14. London Ko - Fatoumata Diawara

(Mali rock, pop) Fatoumata Diawara, known for her embrace of the future as a Malian artist wielding an electric guitar, presents "London Ko," co-produced with Damon Albarn. Her album melds African traditions with Western elements, featuring collaborative tracks that spotlight her powerful vocals and advocate feminist causes. With vibrant tunes and collaborations, Diawara's music champions messages of morality and responsibility while radiating infectious exuberance. Great if you like: Vieux Farka Touré, Tinariwen, Gorillaz, Amadou & Mariam

 
13. AGO - Nana Benz Du Togo

 (music from West Africa, electronic) The music of this quintet from Togo is a fusion of organic African sounds and electronic beats, steeped in voodoo traditions and soulful rhythms. Their songs, aimed at the dance floor, encapsulate a feminist spirit, resonating with the power to both strike with force and tenderly embrace the world's beauty. Great if you like: Songhoy Blues, Oumou Sangaré, Ibibio Sound Machine

 
 
12. Alchemy - Disclosure

(electronic)In "Alchemy," Disclosure returns to their dance music roots, embracing a fresh and vibrant sonic landscape with a focus on club-ready beats and grooves. The album marks their shift away from vocal-heavy pop toward a unified and contemporary dance-driven palette, showcasing a sleek and exhilarating array of rhythmic patterns and synth-rich compositions. Great if you like: Jamie XX, Four Tet, Caribou, Floating Points

 
11. MID AIR - Paris Texas

(rap) "Mid Air," the debut album by Paris Texas, portrays the duo's blend of punk and rap as they envision a future leaving Earth for Mars. Louie Pastel and Felix, the LA-based pair, exhibit their diverse musical influences, spanning from Louisiana rapper Autumn! to Radiohead's acoustic renditions. Known for their eclectic creativity, showcased through music videos themed around space travel, the duo's roots in storytelling and varied interests shape their inventive approach to music. Great if you like: Odd Future, Injury Reserve, Death Grips, BROCKHAMPTON

 
10. SCARING THE HOES - JPEGMAFIA & Danny Brown

(rap, hip hop) What if Danny Brown had joined G-Unit… would he have diverged from label constraints to cultivate his own avant-garde style? His collaboration with JPEGMAFIA on "Scaring the Hoes" merges their shared irreverent humor, pharmaceutical interests, and disdain for mainstream culture, showcasing a blend of glitch-hop and meme-savvy lyricism. Great if you like: Earl Sweatshirt, Run The Jewels, Tyler the Creator, Death Grips

 
9. Black Classical Music - Yussef Dayes

(jazz)Yussef Dayes is a drumming virtuoso known for blending jazz with diverse rhythms. As part of Yussef Kamaal, he left a mark on London's jazz scene with their album "Black Focus." His solo debut, "Black Classical Music," showcases his rhythmic mastery and collaboration with global artists. Great if you like: Thundercat, Kamasi Washington, Shabaka Hutchings, Makaya McCraven

 
8. Robots Playing Into Heaven - James Blake

(hip hop/r&b) James Blake returns to his electronic roots in "Playing Robots Into Heaven," where modular synths birthed dancefloor-friendly tracks with icy melancholy. His innovative use of vocal manipulation and improvisational elements adds texture to the experimental, genre-bending album. Great if you like: Jamie XX, Mount Kimbie, Burial

 
7. Blow Out - Overmono

(electronic) Overmono’s debut album, "Good Lies," defies dance music norms, blending precision and grit, transcending mere functionality with emotive surprises. From globular basslines to glossy, luminous beats, they navigate between symmetry and rough edges, inviting listeners on a vivid, energetic journey through their Welsh upbringing in music. Great if you like: Bicep, fred again…, Caribou, Flume, Mura Masa,

 
6. Echo the Diamond - Margaret Glaspy

(indie rock) In Margaret Glaspy's third album, "Echo the Diamond," she embraces her strengths, crafting a vibrant alt-pop record with compelling songwriting and intimate instrumentation. Her return to guitar-driven sounds creates a dynamic blend of piercing storytelling and infectious melodies. Great if you like: Fiona Apple, Julien Baker, Big Thief

 
5. Sisyphus - Golden Features

(electronic) Golden Features, led by Sydney's Thomas Stell, straddles the line between underground electronic sounds and a growing mainstream presence. With enigmatic allure akin to predecessors like Burial, Stell's music, highlighted by hits like 'Tell Me,' showcases a penchant for privacy and a deliberate focus on sonic artistry over personal exposure. Great if you like: ODESZA, Burial, Flume, Disclosure

 
4. Heavy Heavy - Young Fathers

(pop, electronic, r&b) - In London's Tate Britain, artist Hew Locke's exhibit, "The Procession," served as a muse for Young Fathers' fourth album, "Heavy Heavy." This immersive record invites listeners into a rhythmic, vibrant world of music, echoing the energy of Locke's detailed and colorful artwork. With infectious hooks and a liberated approach to singing inspired by everyday practices in Ghana and Ethiopia, the album pulses with gospel-like affirmations, transcending conventional meaning for joyful resistance and communal engagement. Great if you like: TV on the Radio, Mount Kimbie, Caribou, Perfume Genius

 
3. The Window - Ratboys

(indie rock) "The Window" by Ratboys, produced by Chris Walla, marks a transformative leap for the band, showcasing newfound depth and a fuller sound that amplifies their poignant songwriting. Walla's touch infuses classic-rock grandeur into their alt-pop style, propelling Ratboys into a spotlight that perfectly aligns with the current resurgence of country-tinged indie rock. Great if you like: Waxahatchee, Big Thief, Wednesday, Hop Along

 
2. the record - boygenius

(indie rock) Boygenius, comprising Lucy Dacus, Phoebe Bridgers, and Julien Baker, isn’t just a supergroup, but a redefinition of musical unity. "The Record," strikes me as a watershed moment in music, because despite these mega-stars coming together, their contributions complement one another as a testament to thoughtfulness, mindfulness and kinship. The album is devoid of heavy-handed and indulgent solos or vocal runs you’d expect with a collective. By breaking the norms of band dynamics and inspiring solidarity among women in the music industry, this album revitalizes familiar phrases, expressions, and melodies, reintroducing them in a way that feels utterly fresh. I guess it’s the little things in life that really matter? Great if you like: Japanese Breakfast, Snail Mail, Big Thief, Taylor Swift

 
1. Madres - Sofia Kourtesis

(electronic) I thought I was gonna go boygenius for most of this entire year, but then I heard Sofia Kourtesis's debut album "Madres" and I was placed inside of a mosaic of energizing dance tracks, cultural crossroads, and profound personal narratives. It's a journey through diverse sonic landscapes that blend both poignant storytelling and invigorating beats, crafting a unique space that feels simultaneously familiar and fresh. The album reflects Kourtesis's growth as an artist and her ability to extract new meaning from aged audio fragments, resulting in a vibrant and emotionally charged musical experience. In "Estación Esperanza," Sofia Kourtesis weaves an intricate sonic tapestry by blending raw protest chants from a Peruvian demonstration with fragments from Manu Chao's work. The track's energetic and industrial beats create a compelling backdrop for the rhythmic repetition of a compelling mantra, inviting listeners into a powerful, rallying cry for hope and change."Si Te Portas Bonito" transports you to a sun-kissed dancefloor with its Balearic pop influences and a silky, infectious house beat. Sofia Kourtesis's vocals effortlessly transition between sensuousness and assertion, delivering lyrics that carry both allure and authority, making it a vibrant, multilayered dance anthem. All in all, I am so lucky to have traveled to many places in Latin America this year and this album seems like it captures the breadth of my perspective so well, that I had to make this #1! Great if you like: Jon Hopkins, Boards of Canada, Moby, Aphex Twin, Squarepusher
