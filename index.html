Top Albums of 2022

And here they are, starting with #25. You can click the Album image to get a Spotify/YouTube Link.
25. Mr. Morale and the Big Steppers - Kendrick Lamar

(hip hop) An album likely ahead of its time, filled with emotional storytelling. Kendrick’s candor and complex points demonstrate an even further development for hip hop. Great if you like: Outkast, A Tribe Called Quest, J Cole
 
24. Angels & Queens Part I - Gabriels

(soul) Former American Idol contestant, Jacob Lusk’s harrowing voice on top of Sounwave’s head-bopping beats breathe life into the soul. Great if you like: Frank Ocean, Khalid, Leon Bridges
 
23. Glass Effect - Ben Marc

(jazz) Quite hard to describe, but deeply textured and rhythmically exciting. It takes turns around corners you never would imagine, while keeping calm. Great if you like: Sons of Kemet, Kaytranada, Toro y Moi

22. Everything Was Beautiful - Spiritualized

(rock) Great ready to launch into space once more with Spiritualized. The way they draw out enthusiasm while still holding onto comfortable dad-rock results in harmonious comfortable bliss. Great if you like: Elvis Costello, Jesus and the Mary Chain, Sparklehorse, my bloody valentine

21. LIFE ON EARTH - Hurray for the Riff Raff

(indie rock) Alynda Segarra, a Puerto Rican non-binary person from the Bronx who moved to New Orleans over a decade ago, celebrates the twists and turns of their own life and its collected sounds, reminding us that our own stories are far from simple and far from written. Great if you like: Neko Case, Uncle Tupelo, M. Ward
20. It's Almost Dry - Pusha T

(hip hop) Pharrell and Pusha back again with both of their best work since the Virginia Beach days. The beauty of Pusha is his consistency. His clever lyricism is second to none and his commitment to his identity in an ever-changing world is laudable. Great if you like: Kendrick Lamar, J Cole, Danny Brown, Vince Staples

19. Further Joy - The Regrettes

(pop) - The main narrative here is the Regrettes shed garage punk for a more pop sound, and they couldn’t have done so more perfectly. The song composition still feels punky, but it gives you the serotonin boost of summer bop. Great if you like: Charlie XCX, Kesha
18. Two Ribbons - Let's Eat Grandma

(pop/electronic) There’s something so enchanting about this music that welcomes you to think deeply without feeling like you’re thinking at all. Maybe it's the empty-dance-floor sound of synth-pop. Maybe it’s Jenny’s cleverly musing wordplay. All I know is it makes for a great party for one. Great if you like: Chvrches, Passion Pit
17. Beatopia - beabadoobee

(pop) Filipina-British songwriter Bea Kristi drew on her childhood, feeling like an outsider in a white-dominated world and this album rejects while it creates. Its playful solution to one of the most distressing scenarios is masterful. Great if you like: Sheryl Crow, Olivia Rodrigo

16. Renaissance - Beyoncé

(pop) During the pandemic, we lost dancing, and it’s so very important that culture ambassador Beyoncé be there to remind us in our need to bring it back. Combining elements of dance music throughout history and all over the world, this is one of the most well-crafted pop albums of all time. Great if you like: Janet Jackson, Kylie Minogue, Madonna

15. Pray For Me I Don't Fit In - Melt Yourself Down

(jazz/rock) One of the last installments of the London jazz scene, Melt Yourself Down put forward a riveting and furious, almost early heavy metal, installment of jazz. They pull from classical jazz and afro-beat song structure, resulting in some of the most dynamic music I’ve ever heard. Great if you like: Vulfpeck, Prince, Lettuce

14. If My Wife Knew I'd Be Dead - CMAT

(country/pop) I find it so refreshing to hear something with so much braggadocio yet so much silliness. It’s a healthy confidence. Who knew a young Irish woman would make such perfect country? Great if you like: the Chicks, Meghan Trainor

13. In These Times - Makaya McCraven

(jazz) It’s overwhelming how talented McCavern is. His J-Dilla-like groove seeking pockets and improvisational spontaneity has become signature. Great if you like: Robert Glasper, Ezra Collective, Soulquarians movement
 
12. I Love You Jennifer B - Jockstrap

(electronic/pop) I wouldn’t be surprised if this labyrinthian music isn’t for everyone, but I think the way it shifts from feeling plastic, to metallic, to woodsy, without any conceding is so affirming. You are shapeless and that's what makes you great! Great if you like: Bjork, Fiona Apple, PJ Harvey
11. PAINLESS - Nilüfer Yanya

(pop) This album is venting. It’s raw, unfiltered, chafed pain. It maintains a consistent flow and its reliance on the pure emotion feels almost grunge-like. By the end it does leave the listener with hope that time heals wounds. Great if you like: Sharon Van Etten, Mitski, Waxahatchee

10. Sore thumb - oso oso

(indie rock) The plug and play rock brings back the nostalgia of any high school garage show. It allows itself to be emo without being overbearing. Great if you like: Built to Spill, Pinegrove, Snail Mail, Japanese Breakfast
9. Aboogi - Imarhan

(North African blues) These descendants of the acclaimed desert rock giants Tinariwen have carried the torch beautifully. It is unmistakably Tuareg while also progressive. Great if you like: Ali Farka Touré or Khruangbin

A Light for Attracting Attention - The Smile

(rock) Thom Yorke and Jonny Greenwood from Radiohead and Tom Skinner from Sons of Kemet… I mean this review is practically unnecessary. It does follow some similar album patterns of Radiohead, many avant-garde spiritual tracks, followed by raucous noise rock, followed by passionate piano ballads, but as a move forward not a callback. Amazing that after thirty years, these lads are still pioneering music. Great if you like: Radiohead, Coldplay, Joy Division

7. Dragon New Warm Mountain I Believe In You - Big Thief

(indie rock) This album was recorded in multiple studios across multiple time periods and as a result sounds as if you were picked up by a traveling band on some gleeful pursuit to restore faith in humanity. Great if you like: Lilith Fair artists, Bright Eyes, Death Cab for Cutie

6. Y’all Don’t (Really) Care About Black Women - Melanie Charles

(jazz) This inflamed jazz album boasting proud African swagger is one of the most entertaining albums I’ve ever listened to. It combines urban be-boppy gusto with rural woodsy soothing character. Great if you like: Erykah Badu, Lauryn Hill, Solange
5. KALAK - Sarathy Korwar

(electronic/jazz) I’ll be honest, the heavy-handed intro dissent almost lost me, but the rest of this album is so dang-near perfect, and hits the most core sounds of my identity that I couldn’t leave it off. Indo percussionist, Sarathy Korwar, returns, establishing the place for overtly South Asian sounds in jazz. Great if you like: the XX, Caribou, SBTRKT, James Blake
4. Boat Songs - MJ Lenderman

(indie rock) Fuzzy boastful guitar licks and consistently interesting rhythms make this such a fun album to listen to. It’s alt-country with indie Seattle packaging. Lenderman shows great charm in how simple and honest his lyrics are. Great if you like: Kurt Vile, Bob Dylan, Pavement

3. Three Dimensions Deep - Amber Mark

(r&b/pop) A fresh space-age, sci-fi themed R&B album. It is laid back and swings, making it very easy to listen to. The various samples, eclectic percussion and melodic instruments keep it exciting. Great if you like: SZA, Jhené Aiko, H.E.R.

2. Ants From Up Here - Black Country, New Road

(indie rock) Seeing this theatrical and challenging album performed live may have something to do with how much I love it. This meld of rock, jazz, chamber music comes to life! It’s like a serendipitous combination achieved by otherwise polar dishes on a buffet plate. The rough, rag-tag fashion yearns for early 2000s indie rock, before anything formulaic was developed, and I am for it! Crazy how young these artists are, but maybe that's exactly why. Great if you like: Arcade Fire, David Bowie

1. Un Verano Sin Ti - Bad Bunny

(reggaeton) Anyone who knew me in middle school would know I was obsessed with reggaeton– Wisin y Yandel, Zion y Lennox, obviously Daddy Yankee. I did spend a lot of time jamming out to The Strokes and writing Jay-Z lyrics, but when the first wave of reggaeton came about, despite not being hispanic, I felt spoken to. There were non-English words at the top of the charts! I think I was so protective of that music, that it took me time to warm up to Bad Bunny, but this album is undeniably the best reggaeton album of all time. It has lifted the genre to another strata of art. Its grain and character vary as you wade across its surface. Take for example, Titi Me Pregunto which, at first glance, is playfully poking fun at inquiries by family to your love life– a universal experience. It later resolves into this deep introspective piece about your relationship with romance. It sheds the coat of machismo in earlier reggaeton for something far more real. The few features add specific vital flavors. These proud Puerto Rican anthems demand and are being given respect. Bad Bunny is at the upper echelon of stardom and it’ll be exciting to see what he and whom he’s inspired does next. Great if you like: The Weeknd, Drake, DJ Khaled, Daddy Yankee
